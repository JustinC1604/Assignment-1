Origins

Grasshopper is a Hong Kong Cantopop male group formed in 1985. The group consists of three talented members: Edmond So, Calvin Choy, and Remus Choy. Originally discovered through a talent competition hosted by the legendary Cantopop diva Anita Mui, who became their mentor. The trio rose to fame with their dynamic performances, infectious melodies, and synchronized choreography.

Over the years, Grasshopper has become a household name in the Hong Kong music scene, captivating fans across Asia and beyond with their infectious hits and dynamic stage presence. Their music blends pop, dance, and ballads, creating timeless songs that have stood the test of time.

History and achievements

Grasshopper made their official debut in 1988 with their self-titled album.Their blend of heartfelt ballads and high-energy dance tracks earned them a devoted fan base, and hits like "Ba Ba Ba," "Forget Love Potion," and "Broken Heart Express" cemented their status as Cantopop legends. Their connection to Anita Mui gave them opportunities to shine early in their careers, performing alongside her and receiving invaluable guidance. Grasshopper's unique style influenced the evolution of Hong Kong pop music, incorporating dance choreography into their live performances long before it became mainstream in the industry. In addition to Hong Kong, they gained popularity in Taiwan, China, and Southeast Asia, performing sold-out concerts and releasing albums in Mandarin and Cantonese.

About the Members

Name: Edmund So Chi Wai

Role: Vocalist

Date of Birth 3 May 1966

Bio: Before his debut, So worked in shipping. Later, he formed the band Grasshopper with Calvin Choy and Remus Choy. They participated in the 1985 Rookie Singing Contest, and later developed a singing career with Anita Mui, and acted in TVB TV dramas and hosted the TV show "Entertainment Search". Offstage, Edmund is known for his love of food and family. Married to former singer Winnie Lau since 1998, the couple has two daughters. Edmund's approachable charm and passion for sharing local food spots have endeared him to fans beyond music

Name: Calvin Choy Yat Chi

Role: Lead vocalist and spokesperson

Date of Birth: 3 January 1965

Bio: Calvin Choy Yat Chi is a founding member of the iconic Hong Kong Cantopop group Grasshopper. Alongside his younger brother Remus Choy and childhood friend Edmond So, they formed the group in 1985. Known for his vocal contributions and charismatic stage presence, Calvin played a significant role in shaping Grasshopper's success throughout the late 1980s and 1990s. Beyond music, Calvin is also involved in producing and entertainment projects, solidifying his reputation as a multifaceted entertainer. In addition, Calvin suffers from polydactyly, and has two thumbs on his right hand, with a total of 11 fingers, so he is nicknamed Sir One One (or Sir 11) and Six Fingered Maniac.

Name: Remus Choy Yat Kit

Role: Main dancer and choreographer

Date of Birth: 6 February 1967

Bio: Remus Choy Yat kit is one of the three members of Grasshopper. As the younger brother of fellowbandmate Calvin Choy, Remus initially developed a strong passion for dancing, which inspired the trio's formation. With support from their mother, who was also a dance instructor, Remus encouraged Calvin and childhood friend Edmond So to enter the New Talent Singing Awards competition, which marked the group's debut in the music scene. Over the years, Remus has been known for his vibrant stage presence and creative contributions to the group's music and choreography. In recent years, Remus has also explored his passion for cooking through his successful YouTube channel, Remus Kitchen, where he shares recipes and culinary tips, further showcasing his versatility beyond music
